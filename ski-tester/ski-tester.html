Ski Pressure Testing System

A distributed force measurement system designed to characterize ski camber and pressure distribution under controlled loading.

Problem Statement

Commercial ski fitting and testing tools provide limited quantitative insight into pressure distribution along the ski length. The goal of this project was to design a low-cost, scalable system capable of measuring localized forces across the ski surface while supporting repeatable loading conditions.

System Overview

The system consists of an instrumented mounting frame with 50+ force sensors, signal conditioning electronics, a microcontroller for data acquisition, and a PC-based data analysis pipeline.

Sensor Selection & Trade-offs

Multiple sensing technologies were evaluated, including piezoelectric sensors and strain-gauge-based load cells. Cost, sensitivity at low loads, mechanical robustness, and channel scalability were key constraints influencing the final design.

Electronics & Firmware

Each sensor channel interfaces with an ADC front-end, with multiplexing used to scale the system beyond the native input count of the microcontroller. Firmware handles timed sampling, channel selection, and streaming of calibrated data to a host PC.

Calibration & Validation

Individual sensors were calibrated using known loads to account for sensor-to-sensor variation. Repeatability and hysteresis were evaluated to quantify measurement uncertainty across the system.

Results

The system successfully captured pressure profiles along the ski length, enabling visualization of camber characteristics under different loading scenarios.

Lessons Learned & Future Improvements

  • Improved mechanical isolation between sensor channels
  • Higher-resolution ADCs for low-force measurements
  • Automated calibration routines
